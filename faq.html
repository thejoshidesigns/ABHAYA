Frequently asked questions

Answers, before your first visit.

Practical questions about becoming a patient, scheduling, insurance, treatment, and what to do in a crisis. If you don't see your question, please reach out - we're happy to help.

Getting started

How do I become a new patient?

The easiest way is to complete our online intake form. We'll review your information and reach out within two business days to schedule your first appointment. You're also welcome to call (573) 403-3544 during business hours.

What should I bring to my first appointment?

Please bring a photo ID, your insurance card (if applicable), a list of current medications and doses, and any prior records or evaluations you think would be helpful. If you have a referral, bring that as well. New patient paperwork is sent ahead of time so you can fill it out at your own pace.

What happens at the first visit?

Your first appointment is a comprehensive evaluation. We'll review your history, current concerns, medical and family background, and what you hope to get out of treatment. By the end of the visit, we'll talk through initial impressions and a plan for next steps. There is no pressure to start medication on day one.

How long until I can be seen?

Wait time varies based on demand and the type of care you're looking for. After we receive your intake form, we typically reach out within two business days with scheduling options. If your concern is more urgent, please call us and let us know.

Appointments & logistics

Do you offer telehealth?

Yes. We offer secure video visits for both psychiatry and therapy for patients located in Missouri. You'll need a quiet space, a reliable internet connection, and a device with a camera and microphone. See our telepsychiatry page for the full tech and privacy details.

Which states are you licensed in?

Dr. Yerrapu is currently licensed in Missouri. We can only see patients physically located in Missouri at the time of the visit, including for telehealth. If you live in a neighboring state, we are happy to suggest providers in your area.

How long is a typical session?

An initial psychiatric evaluation is 60 to 75 minutes. Medication management follow-ups are 30 minutes. Therapy sessions are 45 or 60 minutes depending on what you and your clinician agree on. We'll always confirm the length when you book.

What is your cancellation policy?

We ask for at least 24 hours' notice for cancellations. Same-day cancellations or missed visits may be subject to a fee. We understand that things come up - please call us if you need to reschedule and we'll do our best to work with you.

How do I reach you after hours?

We are not able to provide after-hours clinical coverage. For urgent concerns that can't wait, please call 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) or your primary care provider. For emergencies, dial 911 or go to your nearest emergency room.

Do you offer same-day appointments?

Occasionally. We hold a small number of same-day slots for urgent concerns, but they fill quickly. Call us at (573) 403-3544 in the morning to check availability. If you are in crisis, please call, text, or chat 988 instead.

Insurance & cost

What insurance do you accept?

We are in-network with most major plans including Aetna, Anthem Blue Cross Blue Shield, Cigna & Evernorth, Humana, Missouri Medicaid, Medicare, Optum / United Behavioral Health, Tricare, UHC, and UMR. The full list - and instructions for verifying your benefits - is on our insurance page.

Do you offer self-pay rates?

Yes. Self-pay rates are listed on the insurance page. Payment is due at the time of service, and we accept HSA / FSA cards as well as all major credit cards.

What if my plan is out-of-network?

You can still see us. We can provide a superbill for you to submit to your insurance for possible out-of-network reimbursement. Self-pay rates also apply.

Do you accept Medicaid or Medicare?

Yes - both Missouri Medicaid and Medicare are among our accepted plans. Coverage details (copay, prior authorization requirements) depend on your specific plan. We can help you check before your first visit.

Can I use my HSA or FSA card?

Yes. Mental health care is an eligible HSA / FSA expense. We accept HSA / FSA cards as well as all major credit cards at the time of service.

What is a Good Faith Estimate?

Under the federal No Surprises Act, patients who are uninsured or paying out of pocket have the right to a Good Faith Estimate explaining the expected charges for medical items or services over a defined period. You can request one at any time - please ask at the front desk or contact us. The full disclosure is included on our insurance page.

Treatment

Do you prescribe controlled substances?

Yes, when clinically appropriate. This includes stimulant medications for ADHD and certain other controlled medications. We follow careful prescribing protocols, which include regular check-ins, monitoring as needed, and - for some medications - occasional urine drug screens. We do not prescribe controlled substances on a first visit.

How do prescription refills work?

Refills are handled during scheduled visits or by request through your pharmacy. Please request refills at least 48 hours before you run out. If it has been a while since your last appointment, we may ask you to come in before refilling to make sure the medication is still right for you.

Do you see children and adolescents?

We see adolescents (typically age 13 and up) and adults. For younger children, we are happy to help with a referral to a colleague who specializes in that age range.

What kinds of therapy do you offer?

Our approach is supportive and integrative. We draw on evidence-based methods including CBT, DBT-informed skills work, mindfulness-based approaches, and trauma-informed care (including EMDR-informed approaches). The exact approach depends on what you are working on and what fits your life.

Privacy & safety

Is my information confidential?

Yes. We follow HIPAA requirements for protecting your health information. We will not share your information with anyone outside of treatment without your written consent, except in the specific situations required or allowed by law (such as imminent safety concerns). Our full Notice of Privacy Practices explains your rights and our responsibilities in detail.

What do I do in a crisis?

If you are in crisis, call, text, or chat 988 for the Suicide & Crisis Lifeline. It is free, confidential, and available 24/7. For emergencies, dial 911 or go to your nearest emergency room. We are not able to provide emergency or after-hours crisis care.

How do I get a copy of my records?

You can request a copy of your records at any time. Please contact our office and we'll send you the appropriate release form. We process records requests in a timely manner, generally within 30 days.

Do you coordinate with my other providers?

Yes, with your written consent. Coordination of care with your primary care provider, therapist, or other specialists is often an important part of good treatment. We can also reach out to a previous prescriber when helpful.
