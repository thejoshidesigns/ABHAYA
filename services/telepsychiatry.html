How telepsychiatry works

Telepsychiatry is psychiatric care delivered over secure, HIPAA-aligned video. For most visits, it works just like an in-person visit - same time, same conversation, same follow-up. The main difference is that you're on camera from wherever feels most private and comfortable: your home, your car on a lunch break, your office with the door closed.

We use a secure telehealth platform designed for healthcare visits. You don't need to install anything to receive a link. You'll get an email or text with a click-to-join link a few minutes before your appointment.

What it's good for

  • Medication management visits (most follow-ups)
  • Individual therapy sessions
  • Initial psychiatric evaluations (for most patients)
  • Quick check-ins when something has shifted between visits
  • Patients who travel, have childcare constraints, or live outside the Columbia area
Tech requirements

What you need on your end

The technical requirements are minimal - if you can stream a video, you can do a telepsychiatry visit.

Device

Phone, tablet, or computer with a camera and microphone.

Internet

A reliable connection - most home Wi-Fi works fine.

Private space

A room where you can talk freely for the duration of the visit. Headphones recommended.

We send a test link before your first visit to make sure audio and video work. If we run into technical issues, we'll switch to phone and reschedule the video portion as needed.

State licensure

Dr. Yerrapu is licensed in Missouri. To be seen via telepsychiatry, you must be physically located in the state of Missouri at the time of your visit. This is a legal requirement, not a preference. If you travel out of state, we can keep seeing you - but you would need to be in Missouri for the actual appointment.

Privacy and security

We use a HIPAA-aligned video platform. Video is encrypted, not recorded, and not stored. The connection is peer-to-peer where the platform allows. You should still take reasonable steps on your end: use a private space, use headphones if others are nearby, and avoid public Wi-Fi for visits.

Like all healthcare, telepsychiatry has some inherent limits: if you're in crisis and unreachable, we can't be there. If that ever happens, please call 988, 911, or go to your nearest emergency room.

Questions

Frequently asked questions about telepsychiatry

Is telepsychiatry as effective as in-person care?

For most concerns, yes - research consistently shows that telepsychiatry is as effective as in-person care for the majority of conditions. Some things (a careful physical exam, for instance) still require in-person visits, but for therapy and most medication management, video works very well.

Can I switch between in-person and telehealth?

Yes, often. Many patients do a mix: in-person for the first visit, then telehealth for most follow-ups; or in-person during a difficult stretch, then back to telehealth. We're flexible.

What if I have technical problems during a session?

We'll switch to phone. If we can't reconnect within a few minutes, we'll reschedule the video portion. You won't be charged for sessions that fail due to technical issues on our end.

Do you see patients outside Missouri?

Not via telehealth. We are licensed in Missouri only, which means at the time of your visit you must be physically in Missouri. If you're in another state and need a referral, we're happy to help you find someone.

Is telepsychiatry covered by insurance?

Most major insurers cover telepsychiatry the same as in-person care, including Medicare and many Medicaid plans. See the insurance page for the full list of plans we accept.
