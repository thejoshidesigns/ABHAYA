Our commitment

Abhaya Behavioral Health is committed to making this website accessible to everyone, including people with disabilities. We design and build this site to conform with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, Level AA.

What we've done

The following accessibility features are built into every page:

  • Keyboard navigation. Every interactive element — links, buttons, forms, menus — can be reached and used with a keyboard alone. A visible focus indicator shows where you are at all times.
  • Skip link. A "Skip to main content" link at the top of every page lets keyboard users jump past the navigation.
  • Semantic HTML. We use proper HTML5 landmarks (header, nav, main, article, aside, footer) so assistive technology can navigate the page structure.
  • Color contrast. Text colors meet or exceed WCAG AA contrast ratios against their backgrounds.
  • Text alternatives. All meaningful images include descriptive alt text. Decorative images use empty alt attributes.
  • Form labels. Every form field has an associated label, and error messages are announced to screen readers.
  • Resizable text. The site remains usable when you zoom in to 200% or larger, on both desktop and mobile.
  • Reduced motion. Animations respect your system's "reduce motion" preference.
  • Mobile-friendly. The layout adapts to phones, tablets, and desktop screens.

Known limitations

Despite our best efforts, some content may not yet be fully accessible. We're aware of the following and working to address them:

  • Embedded Google Maps. The maps on our contact page are embedded from Google. Their full accessibility is controlled by Google, not by us.
  • Video content. If we add video in the future, we will provide captions and transcripts.
  • PDF documents. If we link to PDF documents (for example, intake forms or policy documents), we will provide accessible HTML versions whenever possible.

This statement is reviewed and updated as we improve the site.

Reporting accessibility issues

If you encounter any accessibility barrier on this site — or if you need information in an alternate format (large print, audio, etc.) — please let us know. We take accessibility feedback seriously and will respond as quickly as possible.

If our response isn't satisfactory

If you contact us about an accessibility issue and feel that we have not responded adequately, you have the right to escalate. You may file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Office for Civil Rights, or with the Missouri Commission for the Deaf and Hard of Hearing, or with the Missouri Department of Mental Health.

If you are in crisis, call, text, or chat 988.

The Suicide & Crisis Lifeline is free, confidential, and available 24/7. For emergencies, dial 911 or go to your nearest emergency room.
